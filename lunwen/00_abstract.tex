\begin{abstract}
Blockchain fraud detection is naturally a temporal graph learning problem, where transaction relations, entity behaviors, and risk propagation patterns evolve over time. In realistic online scenarios, however, maintaining and replaying the full historical graph is often impractical. This paper studies task-only snapshot continual graph fraud detection, where each task can access only the current graph snapshot and cannot store or replay historical nodes, edges, adjacency matrices, or subgraphs. We propose TASD-CL, a Temporal Adaptive Semantic Decomposition framework for continual graph fraud detection. TASD-CL decomposes node representations into normal and abnormal semantic components, performs alpha-guided routing for risk prediction, and preserves fraud-discriminative semantics through three lightweight components: semantic parameter preservation, structure-free prototype consolidation, and semantic conditional distillation. Experiments on Elliptic and Elliptic++ Actor evaluate TASD-CL at transaction and actor/address granularities. Using AUC-ROC and MacroF1 as primary metrics under severe class imbalance, TASD-CL outperforms graph fraud detection baselines and generic continual learning baselines, while ablation results validate the contribution of its semantic preservation components.
\end{abstract}
