\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

This paper studies task-only snapshot continual graph fraud detection for evolving blockchain graphs. Under this setting, future tasks cannot access or replay historical graph structures, while fraud patterns continue to evolve. We propose TASD-CL, a semantic-aware framework built on the TASD semantic decomposition backbone. TASD decomposes node representations into normal and abnormal semantic subspaces, and TASD-CL preserves fraud-discriminative semantics through SSF, SPC, and SCD. Experiments on two blockchain fraud datasets show that TASD-CL improves AUC-ROC and MacroF1 under task-only temporal snapshots. Future work will further study stronger validation protocols across additional blockchain graph sources and broader deployment constraints.
